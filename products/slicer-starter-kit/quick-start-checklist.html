First Print Quick-Start Checklist

Follow this checklist before, during, and after every print. Check the boxes as you go.

1. Pre-Print Checks All Materials

  • Bed is level (paper test or auto-level run within last 5 prints) If prints aren't sticking or first layer looks uneven, re-level first.
  • Build surface is clean (wipe with IPA or warm water)
  • Nozzle is clean and not clogged (extrude 50mm of filament to check flow)
  • Filament loaded and feeding smoothly through the extruder
  • Filament is dry (check for popping/crackling sounds during extrusion) PETG, TPU, and Nylon are moisture-sensitive. Dry before printing if stored open.
  • PTFE tube seated properly (Bowden setups only)
  • Belts are tight (no slack when you pluck them, should "twang")
  • No loose screws on printhead, bed, or frame

2. Slicer Settings to Verify Before You Slice

PLA

  • Nozzle: 205C
  • Bed: 60C
  • Speed: 50 mm/s
  • Fan: 100%
  • Retraction: 5mm
  • Adhesion: skirt

PETG

  • Nozzle: 235C
  • Bed: 80C
  • Speed: 40 mm/s
  • Fan: 40%
  • Retraction: 3.5mm
  • Z-hop: ON (0.2mm)

TPU (95A)

  • Nozzle: 220C
  • Bed: 50C
  • Speed: 20 mm/s
  • Fan: 50%
  • Retraction: OFF
  • Adhesion: brim
  • Correct printer profile selected (check bed size and nozzle diameter)
  • Layer height matches your goal (0.2mm standard, 0.12mm detail, 0.28mm fast)
  • Infill set appropriately (20% general, 40%+ functional, 10% decorative)
  • Supports enabled if needed (overhangs greater than 50 degrees)
  • Preview the sliced file and check first layer, supports, and travel moves

3. First Layer Watch List First 2-3 Minutes

Stay and watch the first layer. Most failed prints show signs in the first minute.

  • Filament is sticking to the bed (not curling up around the nozzle)
  • Lines are slightly squished, not round (round = nozzle too high)
  • No gaps between first layer lines (gaps = nozzle too high or under-extrusion)
  • No filament piling up or dragging (piling = nozzle too low)
  • Skirt/brim looks even and consistent around the full perimeter
  • No weird sounds (clicking = extruder grinding, could be a clog or too cold)
  • Second and third layers go down smoothly on top of the first

4. Mid-Print Checks Optional but Smart

  • No stringing between parts (if yes, check retraction settings for next print)
  • Layers look consistent (no shifting, no gaps, no blobs)
  • Supports are building correctly (if enabled)
  • No layer shifting (horizontal lines not matching up = belt or speed issue)
  • Filament spool is feeding freely (not tangled, not caught on the holder)

5. Post-Print Steps After Completion

  • Let the bed cool before removing the print (especially PEI surfaces)
  • Remove print gently (flex the build plate or use a scraper at a low angle)
  • Remove supports carefully (flush cutters or needle-nose pliers work best)
  • Remove brim/raft if used (snap off, clean edges with a craft knife)
  • Inspect for quality issues and note any changes needed for next time
  • Clean the build surface (remove any leftover filament bits)
  • Store remaining filament in a sealed bag with desiccant

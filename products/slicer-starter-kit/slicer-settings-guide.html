Slicer Settings Guide

The 10 settings that matter most, when to change them, and how to fix common problems

The 10 Most Important Cura Settings

Your slicer has hundreds of settings. Most of them don't matter for everyday printing. These 10 are the ones that actually affect your print quality. Learn these and you'll handle 95% of situations.

1. Layer Height
Cura: Quality > Layer Height

This controls how thick each layer is. Thinner layers (0.12mm) give more detail but take longer. Thicker layers (0.28mm) print faster but look rougher.

When to change it: Use 0.12mm for miniatures and detailed models. Use 0.2mm for general printing. Use 0.28mm when speed matters more than looks.

2. Print Temperature (Nozzle)
Cura: Material > Printing Temperature

Higher temps melt filament more. This improves layer bonding but can cause stringing and oozing. Lower temps give cleaner prints but weaker layer adhesion.

When to change it: Raise by 5C if you hear clicking or see gaps between layers. Lower by 5C if you see strings or blobs. Always check the range on your filament spool.

3. Bed Temperature
Cura: Material > Build Plate Temperature

Keeps the bottom of your print warm so it sticks to the bed. Too low and prints pop off. Too high and you get "elephant foot" where the bottom squishes out.

When to change it: If prints won't stick, raise by 5C. If the first few layers look squished and wider than they should, lower by 5C.

4. Print Speed
Cura: Speed > Print Speed

How fast the nozzle moves while printing. Faster means shorter print times. But going too fast causes ringing, poor layer adhesion, and rough surfaces.

When to change it: Slow down for detailed prints or tricky filaments like TPU. Speed up for large, simple objects where surface finish doesn't matter as much.

5. Retraction Distance
Cura: Travel > Retraction Distance

When the nozzle travels between parts, retraction pulls filament back to stop oozing. Too little and you get strings. Too much and you get jams or gaps.

When to change it: Bowden setups need 4-6mm. Direct drive needs 0.5-1.5mm. If you see stringing, increase by 0.5mm at a time. If you see gaps after travel moves, decrease it.

6. Retraction Speed
Cura: Travel > Retraction Speed

How fast filament gets pulled back. Faster retraction reduces stringing. But too fast can grind the filament or cause jams in the hotend.

When to change it: Bowden: 40-50mm/s works well. Direct drive: 25-35mm/s. Only change this if adjusting distance didn't fix your stringing.

7. Wall Count (Shell Thickness)
Cura: Walls > Wall Line Count

How many outlines the slicer draws for each layer. More walls mean stronger prints and better surface finish. Fewer walls save time and material.

When to change it: Use 3 walls for most prints. Bump to 4-5 for functional parts that need strength. Adding walls is often more effective than increasing infill.

8. Infill Density
Cura: Infill > Infill Density

How much material fills the inside of your print. 0% is hollow. 100% is solid. Most prints don't need more than 20%.

When to change it: Decorative prints: 10-15%. General use: 20%. Functional parts: 30-50%. Going above 60% rarely helps. Add more walls instead.

9. Cooling Fan Speed
Cura: Cooling > Fan Speed

Controls your part cooling fan. More cooling helps PLA overhangs and bridges. But materials like ABS and ASA warp if the fan is on.

When to change it: PLA: 100%. PETG: 30-50%. ABS/ASA/Nylon: 0%. Always start with fan off for the first 2-3 layers to help bed adhesion.

10. Build Plate Adhesion Type
Cura: Build Plate Adhesion > Adhesion Type

Skirt draws a line around your print (just primes the nozzle). Brim adds a flat border that helps the print stick. Raft prints a thick base under everything.

When to change it: Use skirt for most prints. Switch to brim for prints with small bases or materials that warp (ABS, ASA). Only use raft as a last resort. It wastes material and leaves a rough bottom.

Quick Reference: PLA vs PETG vs TPU

Here's a side-by-side comparison of the key settings for each material. These are good starting points for an Ender 3 or similar Bowden printer.

Key Differences to Remember

PETG needs less retraction than PLA. PETG is stringy by nature. Using PLA retraction settings will make it worse because you'll get gaps after travel moves. Use shorter retraction (3-4mm Bowden) and enable Z-hop instead.

TPU needs to go slow. Flexible filament buckles and jams if you push it too fast, especially on Bowden extruders. 20mm/s feels painfully slow, but it's what works. If you have a direct drive upgrade, you can push to 30-35mm/s.

PLA is forgiving. Lean on it while learning. If you're still dialing in your printer, use PLA. It prints at lower temps, handles cooling well, and doesn't need an enclosure. Once you're getting consistent PLA prints, branch out to PETG.

Troubleshooting: If Your Print Looks Like This, Change That

Most print problems come from just a few settings being wrong. Here's a quick reference for the most common issues and their fixes.

Stringing between parts
Fix: Increase retraction distance by 0.5mm. Lower nozzle temp by 5C. Enable Z-hop for PETG. Increase travel speed to 150mm/s.
First layer won't stick
Fix: Level your bed. Slow first layer to 15-20mm/s. Raise bed temp by 5C. Increase first layer flow to 105%. Try a brim.
Rough or blobby surface
Fix: Lower nozzle temp by 5-10C. Check for wet filament (dry it if needed). Slow outer wall speed to 25-30mm/s.
Weak layer adhesion (layers splitting)
Fix: Raise nozzle temp by 10C. Reduce cooling fan speed. Increase wall overlap. Check for drafts hitting the printer.
Warping/curling corners
Fix: Raise bed temp by 5-10C. Add a brim. Use an enclosure for ABS/ASA. Turn off the cooling fan for first 5-8 layers.
Under-extrusion (gaps in walls)
Fix: Raise nozzle temp by 5C. Slow print speed. Check for a clog (do a cold pull). Increase flow rate by 2-3%.
Over-extrusion (thick, uneven lines)
Fix: Lower flow rate by 2-5%. Calibrate your E-steps. Check that your nozzle diameter is set correctly in the slicer.
Ringing/ghosting (wavy pattern on walls)
Fix: Slow print speed to 40mm/s. Reduce acceleration in firmware. Tighten belts. Check for loose screws on the printhead.
Elephant foot (first layer too wide)
Fix: Lower bed temp by 5C. Raise nozzle slightly (your bed might be too close). Reduce first layer flow to 95-98%.
Pillowing (bumps on top surface)
Fix: Increase top layers to 5-6. Increase infill density to 25%+. Make sure cooling is at 100% for PLA.

General Troubleshooting Tip

Change one setting at a time. If you adjust three things at once and the print improves, you won't know which change fixed it. Make one change, print a small test, and evaluate. This feels slower but saves time in the long run.

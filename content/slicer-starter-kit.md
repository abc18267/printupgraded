You bought a 3D printer to make things. Not to spend your weekends adjusting retraction values and Googling why your prints keep failing.

## What Printers Are Supported?

The Cura profiles are built for the **Ender 3 and Ender 3 V2** with a stock Bowden extruder and 0.4mm nozzle. They'll also work well on the Ender 3 Pro and similar Creality printers with the same build volume.

The settings guide and troubleshooting reference work with **any FDM printer and any slicer**. The concepts are the same whether you use Cura, [OrcaSlicer, or PrusaSlicer](/blog/cura-vs-orcaslicer-vs-prusaslicer/). Only the menu locations change.

## How to Use the Profiles

1. Download the ZIP file and unzip it.
2. Open Cura.
3. Go to **Preferences > Configure Cura > Profiles > Import**.
4. Select the `.cfg` file for the filament you want to use.
5. The profile will appear in your profile dropdown. Select it and slice.

That's it. No manual typing. No copying values from a YouTube video.

### What printers are the Cura profiles compatible with?

The profiles are built for the Creality Ender 3 and Ender 3 V2 with a stock Bowden extruder and 0.4mm nozzle. They'll also work on the Ender 3 Pro and similar Creality printers. The settings guide works with any FDM printer.

### Can I use these profiles with OrcaSlicer or PrusaSlicer?

The `.cfg` profile files are for Cura specifically. But the settings guide includes a comparison table with all the key values. You can manually enter them into OrcaSlicer or PrusaSlicer in about 5 minutes. The troubleshooting guide works with any slicer.

### I have a direct drive extruder. Will these profiles work?

The profiles are tuned for Bowden extruders. If you have a direct drive setup, you'll need to adjust the retraction settings. The guide explains exactly what to change: lower retraction distance to 0.5-1.5mm and retraction speed to 25mm/s.

### What version of Cura do I need?

The profiles work with Cura 5.0 and newer. If you're on an older version, update first. It's free.
